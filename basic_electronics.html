Engineering Notation

Scientific notation is the expression of a number n in the form a×10p, where p is a multiple of 3 and a is between 999 and 1. The decimals are usually rounded to the nearest hundreths column.

Examples:

2350 = 2.35 x 10 3
0.0322 = 3.22 x 10 -3
354332.323 = 35.43 x 10 6 (extra digits are rounded)

Engineering Notaion vs Scientific Notation

In electronics and engineering in general, we use what is called the Engineering Notation (as mentioned above). It is a way of writing a number that is commonly confused for Scientific Notation. The difference is, in a×10p for Engineering Notation, p is a multiple of 3 and a is between 999.99 and 1. In Scientific Notation however, (a×10p) p can be any number but a has to be between 9.99... and 1.

Examples:

Engineering:
0.0323 = 32.40 x 10 -3
235000000 = 235.00 x 10 6

Scientific:
0.0323 = 3.23 x 10 -2
235000000 = 2.35 x 10 8

Significant Figures Rules

  • 1. Nonzero numbers (the digits 1 through 9) are always significant figures.
  • 2. A zero used as a place holder between two nonzero digits is considered a significant figure.
  • 3. A zero to the right of the last digit of a decimal is considered a significant figure.
  • 4. A zero used only as a place holder, either on the right or the lest of the decimal poin, if it is not between two nonzero digits, is not      considered a significant figure.
  • 5. When a number is greater than one, a decimal point can be placed following the number to show that all the digits are significant      including the zeros.

    Breadboards

    Breadboards are plastic blocks with a network of inter-connected holes where you would plug in wires to form circuits. the two most upper and lower lines of a bread board are called power termials. Each horizontal line on a power terminal is connected. On the middle of the breadboard, we have the terminal strip. Each vertical line the terminal strips are connected(5 holes), with a break at the center divider.

    Resistors

    Resistor are clay cilinders that reduce the voltage/current in a circuit, making it suitable for a high power source to power a load (lightbulb, toaster, etc.) with a low power tolerance.
